STORIE:🐢 Tami the Brave Turtle


A Pondside Story About Courage, Kindness, and Heart

Near a quiet pond hidden deep in the tall grass, where dragonflies danced in the sunlight and frogs sang in the reeds, lived a little turtle named Tami.

Tami was smaller than the other turtles.
She moved slowly and didn’t say much.
But Tami had something very special — a heart that was big and brave.

A Turtle Like No Other

The other pond animals liked Tami, though they sometimes forgot she was there. She didn’t jump like Frog or soar like Heron. She didn’t play tag or splash loudly in the water.

But Tami didn’t mind.

She liked watching the clouds drift over the water.
She liked listening to the wind rustle the reeds.
And most of all, she liked helping — in quiet little ways.

When a butterfly was stuck in a spiderweb, Tami freed it.
When a lost beetle needed directions, Tami showed the way.
She didn’t do these things for praise — she did them because they felt right.

The Day the Duckling Got Stuck

One very hot afternoon, the pond was buzzing with activity. Animals lounged in the shade or cooled off in the water.

Suddenly, a sharp “peep! peep!” echoed from the far side of the pond.

Everyone turned.

A tiny duckling, fluffy and yellow, had wandered too far from its family and gotten tangled in a patch of thick, sharp reeds.

The duckling flapped its wings in fear and let out a loud cry.

“Help! Peep! Peep!”

The animals gasped.

“Oh no!” squeaked Mouse.
“Those reeds are sharp!” said Frog.
“I don’t want to get poked!” said Hedgehog, backing away.

Everyone watched, but no one moved.

Then — slowly, quietly — Tami began to crawl.

One step at a time, she made her way toward the frightened duckling. Her little legs moved carefully through the grass. Her shell brushed past the cattails. Her eyes stayed focused.

“She’s too small!” whispered one squirrel.
“She’s too slow!” muttered a rabbit.

But Tami didn’t stop.

A Brave Heart in Action

When she reached the duckling, Tami gently used her shell to push the reeds aside.
Then, with a careful tug of her soft mouth, she loosened the last bit of grass wrapped around the duckling’s foot.

The duckling was free!

It gave a joyful peep and nuzzled Tami in thanks.

The animals on the shore cheered as the little duck waddled back to its family.

And in the middle of it all, Tami just smiled — shy, quiet, and proud.

From That Day On...

From that day forward, the animals saw Tami differently.

Not just as the small, quiet turtle by the pond.

But as someone truly brave.

Because Tami had taught them something important:

You don’t have to be loud.
You don’t have to be fast.
You don’t even have to be big.

To be brave, you just need a kind heart and the courage to help

🌠 Moral of the Story:

MORAL: You don’t have to be big or fast to be brave — you just need a kind heart.
